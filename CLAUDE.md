## Common UK Tree Species
- Oak (Quercus) — commonly protected, slow growing
- Ash (Fraxinus) — check for ash dieback (Tree Monkey specialises in this)
- Beech (Fagus) — shallow roots, sensitive
- Silver Birch (Betula) — fast growing, shorter lifespan
- Horse Chestnut (Aesculus) — watch for bleeding canker
- Sycamore (Acer pseudoplatanus) — vigorous grower
- Yew (Taxus) — toxic, slow growing, very long lived
- Cherry/Plum (Prunus) — prone to silver leaf disease
- Pine/Conifer — check for honey fungus
- Lime (Tilia) — common street tree
- Poplar (Populus) — fast growing, invasive roots
- Willow (Salix) — common near watercourses
- Hawthorn (Crataegus) — good for hedging
- Holly (Ilex) — slow growing, evergreen

## Tree Conditions to Flag
- Ash dieback — wilting, blackened shoots, diamond-shaped lesions (Tree Monkey specialises in this)
- Honey fungus — white mycelium under bark, toadstools at base
- Bleeding canker — dark liquid seeping from bark
- Bracket fungi — indicates internal decay
- Dead wood — visible dead branches over paths or buildings
- Leaning trees — sudden lean indicates root failure risk
- Storm damage — split limbs, uprooted sections
